The following graphs will try to illustrate this.

The graphs above and below illustrate the support the British public have for the government. As we can see since roughly summer this year, polling in favour of Boris Johnson and the Conservatives has gone rapidly downhill. Why is this?

First, using the graphs below we explore if the rising deaths caused by Covid-19 have caused this drop in support for the Government. As shown in the graph below there have been significant numbers of deaths with a peak being reached on January 20th 2021 with 1800 deaths.

To explore whether deaths were linked to this drop in Government approval I used a regression where an R squared value of 0.01 was calculated. This shows that deaths have little causation in the drop in Government approval. What else then could be linked to this drop?

In the graph below I regressed Covid cases against Government approval instead, which produced a much higher R squared value of 0.73. This illustrates that there is a significant link between the Government's approval rating and the number of Covid-19 cases in the country.

Leading on from this finding about the link between Government approval and Covid-19 cases, I compared deaths, cases and vaccinations on the graph below to see if the link was made any clearer. We can see again that the darker the circle, the higher up the approval rating is, whilst there appears to be no link between vaccination numbers and deaths to approval.
